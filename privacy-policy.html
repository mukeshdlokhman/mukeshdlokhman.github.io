üìñPrivacy Policy Overview

iOur Commitment: DoctorsMitra is committed to protecting the privacy and security of Protected Health Information (PHI) in accordance with HIPAA, HITECH, and other applicable healthcare privacy regulations.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information and patient health information when you use our healthcare platform. This policy applies to:

  • Healthcare Professionals: Doctors, nurses, and medical staff using the platform
  • Healthcare Organizations: Hospitals, clinics, and medical practices
  • Patients: Individuals whose health information is stored and processed
  • Business Associates: Third parties who have access to PHI

Scope of This Policy

This privacy policy covers:

  1. Collection of Protected Health Information (PHI)
  2. Use and disclosure of health information
  3. Security measures and safeguards
  4. Patient rights under HIPAA
  5. Data retention and disposal practices
  6. International data transfers (where applicable)

üîíHIPAA Privacy Rule Compliance

Permitted Uses and Disclosures

Under HIPAA, we may use and disclose PHI without patient authorization for:

Treatment, Payment, and Healthcare Operations (TPO)

  • Treatment: Providing, coordinating, or managing healthcare and related services
  • Payment: Billing, claims processing, and reimbursement activities
  • Healthcare Operations: Quality assessment, training, and business management

Required Disclosures

We are required to disclose PHI:

  1. To the patient or their personal representative upon request
  2. To the Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) for compliance investigations
  3. When required by law (court orders, subpoenas, public health reporting)

Permitted Disclosures Without Authorization

  • Public Health Activities: Disease reporting, FDA notifications
  • Health Oversight: Audits, investigations, licensing
  • Judicial Proceedings: Court orders, subpoenas
  • Law Enforcement: Legal requirements, victim identification
  • Serious Threats: Preventing imminent harm to health or safety
  • Research: With IRB approval and proper safeguards

Disclosures Requiring Authorization

Patient authorization is required for:

  • Marketing communications (with limited exceptions)
  • Sale of PHI
  • Psychotherapy notes (with limited exceptions)
  • Most uses not covered by TPO or permitted disclosures

‚ö†Ô∏è De-identification: We may use de-identified health information for research, analytics, and AI training. De-identified data has all 18 HIPAA identifiers removed and cannot reasonably be used to identify individuals.

‚è≥Data Retention & Disposal

Retention Periods

We retain health information as required by law and professional standards:

  • Adult Patient Records: Minimum 7 years from last treatment date
  • Minor Patient Records: Until age 25 (or as per state regulations)
  • Financial Records: 10 years for billing and insurance records
  • Audit Logs: 6 years minimum for security and access logs
  • De-identified Data: Retained indefinitely for research and analytics

Secure Disposal Methods

  • Electronic data: Secure deletion using NIST 800-88 standards
  • Physical records: Cross-cut shredding or incineration
  • Storage media: Degaussing or physical destruction
  • Documented disposal certificates for audit purposes

üö®Breach Notification Policy

Definition of a Breach

A breach is defined as the unauthorized acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI that compromises the security or privacy of the information.

Notification Procedures

  1. Investigation: Immediate investigation of suspected breaches within 24 hours
  2. Risk Assessment: Determination if breach poses significant risk
  3. Individual Notification: Written notice to affected individuals within 60 days
  4. HHS Notification: Report to Department of Health and Human Services
  5. Media Notification: For breaches affecting 500+ individuals
  6. Remediation: Implementation of corrective actions

Notification Contents

Breach notifications will include:

  • Description of the breach and information involved
  • Steps individuals should take to protect themselves
  • Description of our investigation and remediation efforts
  • Contact information for questions and assistance

üë∂Children's Privacy Protection

  • United States: Varies by state (typically 13-18)

  • Emergency Treatment: Consent requirements may be waived
